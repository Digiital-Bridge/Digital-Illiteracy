Behavioral Observation

Based on the surveys and our interviews with software developers, we observed several clear patterns about senior citizens and their use of digital technology. Developers highlighted that senior citizens face common challenges such as difficulty understanding complex interfaces, remembering steps, and a general lack of confidence when using digital devices. Technologies like AI and voice assistance were frequently mentioned as solutions that can make usage easier by reducing the need for typing and complicated navigation.

It was consistently observed that seniors should first learn basic digital skills such as making calls, sending messages, and using simple apps before moving to more advanced features. From the surveys, we observed that many seniors struggle with small text, confusing layouts, and too many options in apps, which makes them avoid using technology.

Additionally, both the interviews and survey responses showed that seniors learn best through in-person or blended training methods, as they prefer step-by-step guidance and practical demonstration rather than fully online learning. Overall, we observed that while senior citizens are interested in using technology, they require simpler designs, supportive features, and proper guidance to use it effectively.
